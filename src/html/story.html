Khadija bint Khuwaylid (RA)

Khadija (RA) was the first wife of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) and the first to believe in his message. A successful merchant and a trusted companion, she supported the Prophet during the earliest years of revelation.

Brief timeline

  • c. 555 CE — Approximate birth of Khadija bint Khuwaylid.
  • c. 595 CE — Marriage to Muhammad (PBUH).
  • 610 CE — First revelations begin; Khadija is the first believer.
  • 619 CE — Death of Khadija (RA); the period following is known as the "Year of Sorrow".

Her role and impact

Khadija (RA) was more than a spouse: she offered emotional strength, financial support and social standing that enabled the early Islamic message to survive its earliest pressures. Her belief in Muhammad (PBUH) came at a time when he lacked social allies; her faith and status were foundational.

Death and aftermath

Khadija passed away in Mecca and was buried in Jannat al-Muʿalla. Her death, followed soon after by the passing of Abu Talib, created the period known as ʿĀm al-Ḥuzn — the Year of Sorrow. Contemporary sources describe the Prophet's profound grief and social isolation, but also his enduring reverence for Khadija.
